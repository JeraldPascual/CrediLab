# Build Sustainable Upgrades (BSU) Hackathon Mechanics
"Innovating Today for a Sustainable Tomorrow"

## I. Overview
The Hackathon is a time-bound innovation competition exclusively designed for Bachelor of Science in Information Technology (BSIT) and Bachelor of Science in Data Science (BSDS) students. The activity aims to promote collaborative problem-solving and the development of technology-driven solutions aligned with the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). Participants will conceptualize, design, and develop a functional prototype within a defined development period.

## II. Objectives
The Hackathon aims to:
* Encourage BSIT and BSDS students to apply technical and analytical skills to real-world problems
* Promote innovation aligned with the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)
* Enhance teamwork, creativity, and interdisciplinary collaboration
* Develop practical, feasible, and socially relevant technological solutions

## III. Eligibility
* The Hackathon is open exclusively to currently enrolled BSIT and BSDS students.
* Participants must be officially enrolled during the current academic term.
* Each participant may join only one team.

## IV. Team Composition
* Each team shall consist of two (2) to three (3) members only.
* Team members may be from the same or different year levels, provided they belong to the BSIT or BSDS programs.
* Each team must designate one (1) Team Leader, who will serve as the official representative.

## V. Registration
* Teams must complete the official registration process within the announced deadline.
* All submitted information must be accurate and complete.
* Incomplete or late registrations will not be accepted.

## VI. Theme and SDG Alignment
* All projects must be aligned with at least one (1) United Nations Sustainable Development Goal (SDG).
* The specific SDG(s) addressed must be clearly identified and justified in the project proposal and final presentation.
* Solutions should demonstrate clear social, environmental, or economic impact relevant to the chosen SDG.

## VII. Development Period and Timeline
The official development period will run from February 1, 2026 to March 5, 2026.

**Hackathon Timeline:**
* **February 2** – Official Kick-off, Orientation, and Announcement of Guidelines (1:00 PM, Room 206)
* **February 2–25** – Project Development and Mentoring (if applicable)
* **February 16** – Checkpoint 1 (Online) (Purpose: to monitor project progress)
* **February 23** – Checkpoint 2 (Online) (Purpose: to monitor project progress)
* **February 26** – Final Submission of Outputs
* **March 5** – Project Presentation, Demo, and Judging

*No submissions will be accepted beyond the stated deadlines.*

## VIII. Development Guidelines
* All solutions must be developed within the official development period.
* Use of open-source tools, libraries, frameworks, and datasets is allowed, provided proper acknowledgment is given.
* Any pre-existing code or resources must be disclosed during submission.
* Teams must ensure originality and avoid plagiarism.

## IX. Expected Output and Submission Requirements
Each team is required to submit the following:

1.  **Functional Prototype or System**
    *   A working application, system, model, or tool (web-based, mobile, or desktop)
    *   Demonstrates core features addressing the chosen SDG
2.  **Project Documentation (PDF)**
    *   Project title and team details
    *   Problem statement and SDG addressed
    *   Proposed solution and system overview
    *   Tools, technologies, and methodologies used
    *   Expected impact and beneficiaries
3.  **Presentation Deck (Slides)**
    *   Overview of the problem and SDG alignment
    *   Solution features and workflow
    *   Demo screenshots or live demo
    *   Impact, feasibility, and future enhancements
4.  **Source Code / Model Files**
    *   Uploaded to a shared repository or storage platform, as instructed by the organizers

## X. Presentation and Demo
* Each team will be given a fixed time to present and demonstrate their project.
* The presentation must clearly explain the problem, solution, SDG alignment, and impact.
* Failure to comply with the time limit may result in score deductions.

## XI. Scoring Rubric
Projects will be evaluated based on the following criteria:

| Criteria | Description | Percentage |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **SDG Relevance & Impact** | Alignment with SDG and societal relevance | 25% |
| **Innovation & Creativity** | Originality and uniqueness of the solution | 20% |
| **Technical Implementation** | Correctness, efficiency, and use of appropriate technologies | 25% |
| **Functionality & Usability** | System performance and user experience | 15% |
| **Presentation & Documentation** | Clarity, organization, and communication | 15% |
| **Total** | | **100%** |

## XII. Judges and Deliberation
* Projects will be evaluated by a panel of judges appointed by the organizers.
* The judges’ decisions are final and binding.

## XIII. Awards and Recognition
* Certificates and awards will be given to top-performing teams.
* All participants will receive a Certificate of Participation.

## XIV. Code of Conduct
* Participants must observe professionalism, respect, and ethical behavior.
* Any form of misconduct, plagiarism, or rule violation will result in disqualification.
